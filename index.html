The Goonies

The Goonies is a 1985 classic American adventure comedy film from the story of Steven Spielberg.

Facing foreclosure of their homes in the "Goon Docks" area of Astoria, Oregon, to an expanding country club, a group of children who call themselves "The Goonies" gather for one final weekend together. The children come upon an old treasure map hidden in Mikey and Brands' parent's attic and soon find themselves on a quest to hunt down the treasure and infamous pirate by the name of "One-Eyed Willy", believed to be located somewhere nearby.


Join Mikey and Brand, and their friends Data, Mouth, Andy and Stef as the children embark on a journey that takes them through multiple caves and tunnels throughout Astoria. Unfortunately, Chunk, one of the goonies, was captured by the Fratellis, a mother and her two sons, a trio of criminals. While being held captive in an abandoned restaurant that served as their outpost, Chunk eventually met and befriended a third son of the Fratellis, a disfigured human by the name of Sloth, who was kept shackled in the basement.

Along the way the group of children evade various booby traps and the Fratellis, who through Chunk, find out about the same treasure. Watch this adventure to find out if the children ever discover "One-Eyed Willy" and if the rumors of the treasure are true. Beware of the Fratellis, as they will be on your trail every step of the way.

Starring
Sean Astin as Mikey, Josh Brolin as Brand, Jeff Cohen as Chunk, Corey Feldman as Mouth, Kerri Green as Andy, Martha Plimpton as Stef, and Ke Huy Quan as Data. Supporting roles include John Matuszak as Sloth, Robert Davi as Jake Fratelli, Anne Ramsey as Mama Fratelli, and Joe Pantoliano as Francis Fratelli.
